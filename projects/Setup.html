Why W10?

Buying a Thinkpad, then installing Windows 10 seems to be the most contradictory thing; so why? The answer is my NVidia GPU. I labored much in vain to get it to work on Arch, but to no avail. If you have one of these old Thinkpads with GPUs, you're not going to get it to work on Linux perfectly. I knew that, but I couldn't get it to work at all. There are a number of problems: first is determining your driver version. According to the NVidia site, my K1000m supports the 470xx driver. But that's wrong. In 'reality', and as my WM recommended, I have to use the 390xx (Fermi) drivers; I suspect this might be for OEM reasons. Anyways, I tried out the 470xx drivers unaware, and they failed. I then tried the 390xx driver... but that too failed. Why? I still haven't figured it out. I decided to uninstall Arch and use Pop!_OS (renowned for its NVidia support), but that was an even worse experience. For starters, neither the 390xx nor the 470xx drivers came installed (even with the NVidia ISO). But they couldn't be installed without downgrading the kernel; and that wasn't possible without major headaches on Pop!_OS. So, after a couple days of messing around, I installed W10 and voila, it worked! I plan to shift to Linux maybe once W10 support is withdrawn, but until then...
